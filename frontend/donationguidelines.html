Donation Guidelines

Please follow these recommendations to ensure your food donations are safe, nutritious, and beneficial to our community.

  • Food Safety

    Ensure all food items are fresh, properly stored, and within their expiration dates. Food that is spoiled, moldy, or past its prime should not be donated.

  • Packaging

    Package food securely to avoid spoilage or contamination during transportation. Use clean, food-safe containers and ensure they are properly sealed.

  • Perishable vs. Non-Perishable

    Clearly label perishable items with dates, and prioritize non-perishable items when possible. Perishable items should be refrigerated promptly.

  • Accepted Items

    Donate fruits, vegetables, dairy products, canned goods, and other nutritious options. Avoid items that are expired, opened, or improperly stored.

  • Donor Responsibilities

    Follow local health standards and guidelines, and keep a record of your donations for tracking purposes. This helps us maintain quality and safety.

  • Contact Information

    Provide accurate contact details so we can reach out if additional information is required about your donation.

Need More Information?

If you have any questions regarding these guidelines or need further assistance, our support team is here to help.
